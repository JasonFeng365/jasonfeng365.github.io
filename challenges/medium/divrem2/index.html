You are given a list of pairs of prime numbers. Each pair is a divisor and a remainder, in the order of increasing divisors. You must find the smallest positive number that, when divided by each divisor, has a remainder that matches the remainder in the pair.
This time, the first divisors will be small like before, under 50. The last number, however, will be large, between the range of 10 thousand and 20 thousand. For example:

7 4
11 8
29 20
12959 11054

Given this list, 26926897 should be output. The resulting number should have a remainder of 4 when divided by 7, a remainder of 8 when divided by 11, a remainder of 20 when divided by 29, and a remainder of 11054 when divided by 12959. 26926897 is the smallest number which satisfies these conditions.

Find the smallest positive number that works for the following set of numbers. The number will be less than 20 billion.

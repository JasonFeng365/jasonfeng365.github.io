Although I dreamed of this one, it seems to be inspired by the Spring 2023 PacNW ICPC's D2 Problem J: Sun and Moon. Try to solve that one, too!

You are given a list of pairs of prime numbers. Each pair is a divisor and a remainder, in the order of increasing divisors. You must find the smallest positive number that, when divided by each divisor, has a remainder that matches the remainder in the pair. For example:

7 3
11 4
29 22

Given this list, 1907 should be output. The resulting number should have a remainder of 3 when divided by 7, a remainder of 4 when divided by 11, and a remainder of 22 when divided by 29. 1907 is the smallest number which satisfies these conditions.

Find the smallest positive number that works for the following set of numbers. The number will be less than 1 million.
3 1
11 4
19 14
23 0
29 27
31 16

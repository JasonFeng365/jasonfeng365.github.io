Prime ID

Find all the factors of a very large number.

For a positive integer, its prime factorization is a list of all the prime numbers, which when multiplied together, results in the original integer. For example, the prime factorization of 90 is [2, 3, 3, 5], because 2 * 3 * 3 * 5 is 90.

That number's prime ID is the sum of each factor, multiplied by that factor's frequency. For 90, the prime ID is calculated as such:
(2 * 1) + (3 * 2) + (5 * 1)
2, 3, and 5 are from the prime factorization. In said factorization, 2 occurs once, 3 occurs twice, and 5 occurs once, so 2 is multiplied by 1, 3 by 2, and 5 by 1. Then, all of these are added together for a final answer of 13.

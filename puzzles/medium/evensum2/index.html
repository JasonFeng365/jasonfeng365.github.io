Even Sum 2

This puzzle is very similar in concept to Odd Sum. As with Odd Sum, try and find the O(1) solution.
Hint: don't use a loop.

For a positive integer n, its even sum is the sum of every even integer less than or equal to n.
For example, given n=6, 12 should be returned (2 + 4 + 6), and given n=5, 6 should be returned (2 + 4).

Given a list of positive integers, output the sum of their even sums. For example:
3
4
5
6

Given this list, 26 should be output. The even sum of 3 is 2, the even sum of 4 is 6, the even sum of 5 is 6, and the even sum of 6 is 12. Adding these together yields 26.
The large input set will result in a sum that is very large. To keep the output small, take the remainder of the output divided by 1000007 (10^6 + 7). The maximum value of each n is 100,000,000.

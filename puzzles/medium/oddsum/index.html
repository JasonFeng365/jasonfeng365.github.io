Odd Sum

This puzzle is originally from a friend of mine's MATLAB assignment. I changed it to be more competition-like. Try and find the O(n) solution.

For a positive integer n, its odd sum is the sum of every odd integer less than or equal to n.
For example, given n=5, 9 should be returned (1 + 3 + 5), and given n=4, 4 should be returned (1 + 3).

Given a list of positive integers, output the sum of their odd sums. For example:
3
4
5
6

Given this list, 26 should be output. The odd sum of 3 is 4, the odd sum of 4 is 4, the odd sum of 5 is 9, and the odd sum of 6 is 9. Adding these together yields 26.
The large input set will result in a sum that is very large. To keep the output small, take the remainder of the output divided by 1000007 (10^6 + 7).
